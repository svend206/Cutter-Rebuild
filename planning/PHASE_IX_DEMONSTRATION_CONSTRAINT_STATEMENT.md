# Phase IX â€” Demonstration Constraint Statement

## 1. Scope and Non-Claims
This statement constrains demonstrations. It does not authorize claims and does not imply correctness, safety, protection, or competence.

## 2. Demonstrations MAY Show
- Recorded data as stored, without implying correctness.
- Refusal behavior as refusal, without implying prevention or capability.
- Absence or uncertainty as absence or uncertainty, without mitigation.

## 3. Demonstrations MUST NOT Show
- Any narrative implying detection, prevention, or control of abuse or failure.
- Any narrative implying correctness, completeness, durability, availability, ordering, or security.
- Any narrative implying fitness for purpose, safety, protection, or harm reduction.
- Any narrative implying outcomes, success, or suitability.
- Any narrative implying trustworthiness or enterprise readiness.

## 4. Required Visibility
Demonstrations MUST keep the following visible:
- refusal when preconditions are not met
- uncertainty when facts are not known
- absence when data is missing

## 5. Prohibited Framing
Demonstrations MUST NOT rely on:
- testimonials or case studies
- comparative claims against industry norms
- euphemisms that soften denials
