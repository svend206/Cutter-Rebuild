# Phase IX â€” Prohibited Claim List

## 1. Scope
This list enumerates claims that MUST NOT appear in marketing, sales, demos, or contracts. It includes explicit and implied claims.

## 2. Prohibited Claims (Non-Exhaustive, Binding)
The following statements are forbidden:
- Any statement that implies correctness, completeness, durability, availability, ordering, consistency, or security.
- Any statement that implies detection, prevention, mitigation, or control of abuse or failure.
- Any statement that implies fitness for purpose, safety, protection, or harm reduction.
- Any statement that implies confidentiality, privacy, or access protection.
- Any statement that implies reliability, robustness, or enterprise-grade readiness.
- Any statement that implies outcomes, success, or suitability.
- Any statement that implies the system knows, decides, or guarantees.

## 3. Prohibited Framing and Terms
The following terms and framings are prohibited:
- "ensures"
- "helps guarantee"
- "designed to prevent"
- "reduces risk"
- "improves safety"
- "trusted"
- "reliable"
- "enterprise-grade"
- "best-in-class"
- "robust"
- "secure"

## 4. Prohibited Implications by Example
The following are prohibited:
- Testimonials, case studies, or examples that imply outcomes or competence.
- Comparisons to industry norms that imply missing claims.
- Silence or omission used to imply a positive claim.

## 5. Enforcement Rule
If a statement answers "Why should I trust this system?", it is prohibited.
